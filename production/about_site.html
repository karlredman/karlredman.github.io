Why does this site use static pages?

  • I built this website using static pages for portability reasons. Not software portability, mind you, but so that I can carry the site with me and post it anywhere as needed.

Why do you have links sprinkled about in the 'Work Related Skills' sections of the site?

  • This is a work in progress (WIP). I intend to eventually provide links for every aspect of these sections. This is so that visitors to the site know exactly what I mean but use of the terms referred to therein.

Why do your lists move around unevenly throughout the site (i.e. the lists in the 'Work Experience Summary' on the home page)?

  • In an effort to provide maximum readability for the various content of the site I've elected to contain each list within a parent container (usually a table). This allows for adaptation of the individual lists to occur as needed relative to screen/browser resolution. This was a design choice to provide maximum readability across as many screen (read as 'device') types as possible. You may notice that the lists from the various sections on these pages move around dynamically but they retain their properties as lists. Again, this is by design, for better or worse.

Why are you running your website from your home IP?

  • I don't want to spend the money right now to host a website from a remote location.
  • I have better control over the in's and out's of data from here for now.
  • I have an agreement with my service provider that lets me do this.
  • I have backup systems in place that I can point the DNS to at any time :D
